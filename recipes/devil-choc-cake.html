Devil's Chocolate Cake

Description

"Devil's Chocolate Cake" or known as "Devil's Food Cake" in the United States of America, is a moist, rich chocolate cake that is layered with a rich buttercream in the center. It was invented in the US in 1905.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup of butter
  • 3 (1 ounce) squares of unsweetened chocolate
  • 2 cups of white sugar
  • 1 cup of water
  • 2¼ cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons of baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoons of salt
  • ¼ cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoons of distilled white vinegar

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease and flour two 9" round pans or one 9x13" pan
  2. In a small pan, melt butter or margerine with the unsweetened chocolate. Set to one side to cool slightly
  3. Cream together sugar and eggs until light in color. Add the chocolate mixture to the eggs and temper the mixture by beating well. Add 1 cup of boiled water (still warm) and blend well. Mixture will be very liquid.
  4. Mix the flour, baking soda, and salt. Add this mixture to the chocolate mixture and blend well.
  5. Mix together vinegar and the milk and stir into the chocolate batter. Pour into the pans that we prepared earlier.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick or a knife that is inserted in the center comes out clean, about 30 minutes.
  7. Serve with custard or serving cream.

Tips

  • This cake is delicious layered with mousse, chocolate ganache, or Black Forest Fillings
