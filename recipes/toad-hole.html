Toad in the Hole

Description

Toad is a traditional English dish that is made up of a Yorkshire Pudding batter and sausages. The dish has been around since 1762 where it was described as a "vulgar" name for a "small piece of beef baked in a large pudding" (Source: Wikipedia) Now, it is well-loved across the United Kingdom.

Ingredients

For the Batter

  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ teaspoon himalayan rose pink salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 pinches of cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup of cold water

For the sausages

  • ¼ cup Filippo BerioTM Olive Oil
  • 4 Cumberland Sausages

For the Onion Gravy

  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 large red onion, minced
  • kosher salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • ¼ teaspoon of Worcestershire SauceTM
  • 1 teaspoon of balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoon of onions, spring or scallions (includes tops and bulb), raw
  • 1 teaspoon of chopped fresh chives, or to taste

Method

  1. Prepare the batter Combine the eggs, salt, pepper, cayenne, 1 cup plus 1 tablesppon of flour, and milk into a mixing bowl; whisk thoroughly until the batter is very smooth and there are no lumps remaining. Whisk in the water. Cover with a teatowel and let it rest at room temperature for one hour.
  2. Prepare the sausages Heat the Filippo BerioTM Olive Oil in a skillent over a medium-high heat. Add the sausages and cook until browned, 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from the heat and transfer to a buttered 9x12 or 9x13" baking dish. Pour the oil from the skillet over the sausages and brush the oil with a pastry brush over the bottom and up the sides of the dish.
  3. Start the onion gravy Melt the butter in the sillet that we were just using, over a medium-high heat. Add the onion and salt and saute until soft and starting to brown. Time: 5-6 Minutes
  4. While the onion is cooking pour the batter in and around the sausages. Place in the center of a cold oven and turn the temperature to 500°F (260°C). Set the oven timer for 30 minutes.
  5. While the toad in the hole cooks, finish the onion gravy Stir in the flour and cook for about 2 minutes. Whisk in the chicken broth and return to a simmer. Add the Worcestershire sauce and the balsamic vinegar and simmer until the gravy thickens up and reduces, about 5 minutes. Reduce the heat to low and sir in the green onions. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. When timer goes off remove toad in the hole from the oven and it should be browned and puffy. Spoon the gravy over the sausages and garnish with green onions and chives.
  7. Serve with extra gravy and mash potatoes and vegetables.
